Late-completion liquidated damages calculator

If your developer delivers vacant possession after the date in your Sale & Purchase Agreement, the standard S&P prescribed by the Housing Developers Rules entitles you to liquidated damages at 10% per annum on the instalments you have paid, computed daily, until vacant possession is delivered.

How the number is derived

Daily LD = instalments paid × 10% ÷ 365. Total LD = daily LD × number of days between the contractual vacant possession date and actual delivery. Example: if you have paid S$900,000 (typically ~60% of the price by this stage) and delivery is 90 days late, LD ≈ S$246.58/day ≈ S$22,192.

Practical notes before you claim

Check your actual S&P dates. Developers build buffers into the contractual date — a project delivering "later than the marketing brochure said" is often still within contract. LD runs only from the contractual vacant possession date.

The claim is contractual and self-executing. The standard S&P obliges the developer to pay; in practice you (or your solicitor) write to the developer's solicitors. Many developers offset LD against the final payments due from you.

Deviations need approval. The standard S&P terms can only be varied with the Controller of Housing's approval, so an S&P without the 10% clause is rare — but verify yours.
