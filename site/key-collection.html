Key collection day, done properly

Once TOP is issued, your developer issues a Notice of Vacant Possession and the 25% completion payment falls due. Here is the sequence that protects you.

Before the appointment

  • Funds in place: your solicitor and bank settle the 25% TOP instalment (CPF/loan disbursement takes a few days — don't leave it to the last minute).
  • Fire & home insurance: arrange coverage from the handover date; mortgagee banks require it.
  • Utilities: open an SP account effective from collection day.
  • Book an inspection slot you can use fully — bring a phone for photos, a charger for testing sockets, painter's tape to mark defects, and patience.

At handover

  • Verify the unit against the S&P specification list (fittings, appliances, finishes).
  • Test every tap, drain, window, door, socket, aircon mode and water heater.
  • Photograph and log every defect — floors, walls, joinery alignment, ponding on balconies, hollow tiles.
  • Submit the developer's defects form in writing and keep a copy. Verbal reports are not a record.

The 12-month defects liability period

From the date of vacant possession you have 12 months during which the developer must rectify defects at their cost. The strongest position comes from a thorough, documented inspection in the first weeks — before renovation muddies causation. Many buyers engage an independent defect inspection firm (market rate roughly S$200–400 for a typical unit) for a systematic report; it is optional, and most valuable for larger units or where you plan heavy renovation.

Renovating a just-TOP'd unit

  • MCST/developer house rules apply from day one — hacking windows, always approvals.
  • Anything structural needs a PE and permits; unauthorised works in a pre-CSC building can hold up the whole project's CSC and land back on you.
  • Keep renovation off defective areas until the developer has rectified and you've signed off — rectification after renovation becomes a fight about who caused what.
General information only. Your S&P, your bank's terms and the development's house rules govern. Last verified July 2026.
