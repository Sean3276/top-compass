The Completion Gateway Playbook

Planning, tracking and clearing Singapore's TOP and CSC — every agency, every form, every service standard, and a working master schedule. For Qualified Persons, developer project managers and submission consultants.

Edition 1.1 — July 2026. Covers the CORENET 2 (ESPro) and CORENET X regimes as at July 2026, including the 3 Jul 2026 e-form cutover and circulars through BC-2025-01.
Licensed to the purchasing organisation for internal project use. Not for redistribution or resale. The programme planner & tracker is built into this document (Part 6) — it works offline in your browser and your project data never leaves your machine.

How to use this playbook

This document does three jobs: it tells you what must be cleared (Parts 2–5), when to start each item (Part 1, the reference master schedule), and how to plan and track a live project (Part 6, the interactive programme & tracker — anchor it on your target TOP or CSC date and it back-computes the whole programme, with every lead time adjustable and progress saved in your browser).

Every duration in this playbook is labelled by its basis, because they are not equally trustworthy and a professional schedule should not pretend otherwise:

SLA statutory or regulatory requirement STD agency-published service standard TYP practitioner-typical planning estimate — confirm for your project wd = working days; cd = calendar days. SP Group items are quoted in "business days" mirroring SP's own wording
Which regime are you in? Projects submitted under CORENET 2.0 close out via the TOP/CSC portal and ESPro e-forms (mandatory since 3 Jul 2026; Word forms sunset 2 Jul 2026). New projects ≥30,000 m² GFA since 1 Oct 2025 — and ALL new projects from 1 Oct 2026 — run through CORENET X, where completion-stage technical clearances are sought concurrently at the Completion Gateway and the overall TOP/CSC application is filed by the appointed Project Coordinator. Ongoing projects onboard from 1 Oct 2027 with approvals carried over. The clearances themselves are the same in both regimes; the portal and sequencing freedom differ.

Part 2 — Agency clearance cards

SCDF — Temporary Fire Permit / Fire Safety Certificate

Needed at TOP (TFP acceptable where only very minor items outstanding; FSC by CSC)
Chain fire systems T&C → RI(A)/RI(M&E) inspection → Form 1/2 + report → CORENET application
Standards application response 3 wd STD; audit inspection normally within 10 days STD; TFP validity ≤6 months STD; end-to-end from RI certification 2–6 weeks TYP
Kills schedules missing Certificates of Conformity for regulated fire products (fix at procurement); deviations discovered by the RI on site; purpose-group misclassification

PUB — three clearance streams

Needed at TOP (all three: sanitary/sewerage COMPOFWORK · surface drainage DRATOP + handing-over · water supply)
Standards TOP/CSC clearance 14 wd STD; LP certifies water service work within 7 days of completion STD; compliance certificates within 2 years STD (anchor date — TOP vs PUB clearance — unconfirmed; verify with PUB)
Note water turn-on gates fire-main commissioning — sequence it at T−90, not with the paperwork at T−45

NEA — environmental health

Needed at TOP · largely document/photo-based · apply ~T−40

URA — planning-condition compliance

Needed at TOP · service standard 7 wd STD
Kills schedules deviations from the Written Permission surfacing at completion. Audit at T−150; waivers (reported ~S$300/item — verify current DC fee schedule) are a design-stage tool — there is no waiver-at-TOP mechanism

SP Group / SP Services (MSSL) — power and unit inspections

Standards service cable 4–6 weeks from switchboard readiness STD; substation handover 10 weeks pre-energisation STD; LEW energisation booking 14 business days STD; unit turn-on appointments ~7 business days after account opening STD
Gates LEW Certificate of Compliance + Certificate of Readiness + EMA electrical installation licence + SP inspection PASS
Residential >10 units successful SP unit electrical inspections are a TOP/CSC prerequisite (APPBCA-2018-01); the completion-notice document is administered by SP — confirm the current form with SP Services. Batch inspections from T−60

IMDA — COPIF joint inspection

Needed COPIF requires telecom facilities (MDF room, lead-in pipes, risers) ready and handed over to licensees no less than 6 months before TOP STD — the earliest hard deadline; enforced under the Telecommunications Act, outside the TOP portal entirely. Schedule the joint inspection to meet it; note COPIF is under review (2026 consultation)

LTA — street works, parking, rail protection

Needed at CSC (not TOP) · 20 wd end-to-end including inspections STD

NParks — greenery and landscape

Needed at CSC (not TOP) · inspection within 7 wd of confirmation, response within 7 wd STD

Part 3 — BCA internal sub-clearance cards

Lift & escalator Permits to Operate (LEAP)

Standards PTO application 7 wd STD; end-to-end from commissioning 2–3 weeks TYP; PTO valid 12 months, renewable from 3 months before expiry SLA
Since 1 Oct 2025 QP certification of fixed installations is a PTO prerequisite, and QP/SPE must be physically present for all acceptance testing SLA
Upstream Cat A fixed-installation plan approval (min 15 wd, plan 3–4 weeks + resubmission rounds) gates installation itself — clear it by T−270

Green Mark / environmental sustainability (≥2,000 m² GFA)

Statutory as-built score (BPD_GM02, GM e-filing) lodged before the TOP application SLA
Certification track (if pursuing a GM rating): assessment ~6 weeks, as-built verification 3–4 weeks, award ~3 weeks — 4–6 months end-to-end TYP; run it parallel, not on the critical path

Buildability (≥5,000 m² GFA)

Standard as-built B-score/C-score jointly declared by architectural + structural QPs; submit ~2 months before the TOP application STD

CD shelters (household/storey)

Standards commissioning booking ≥5 wd ahead STD; failed commissioning must be re-applied for ≥14 wd before the proposed new date STD — budget one failure loop

CONQUAS / Quality Mark — the inspection gate

Since 1 Jun 2025 BCA will not proceed with the TOP/CSC inspection until assessments are complete and site conditions acceptable (APPBCA-2024-20) STD
Mechanics weekly booking window (Tue 08:00–Wed 23:59, one week ahead); half-day assessment blocks; cancel ≥3 wd ahead or be penalised; waterponding method statement to BCA ≥2 weeks before tests STD

Accessibility / Universal Design + structural closeout

Checked at the TOP inspection itself; incomplete accessibility work is an "unacceptable site condition" that aborts it
Closeout papers Certificates of Supervision, QP declaration, Builder's Certificate of Completion, lightning protection and ACMV supervision certificates, as-built structural plans

Part 4 — The TOP application week

  1. Request the inspection (T−21). BCA proposes a date within 2 working days; you must confirm no later than one week before the proposed date or the request lapses (practitioner-observed portal behaviour). TYP
  2. Lodge the forms (T−5). Certificate of Supervision of Building Works + Declaration by QP — ESPro e-forms since 3 Jul 2026 (Word forms sunset 2 Jul 2026); CORENET X projects file at the Completion Gateway. Rule: not less than 3 days before inspection; T−5 guarantees ≥3 working days on any weekday. STD
  3. Pass the site test. Safe access, completed safety and accessibility works, no major liveability defects, CONQUAS done — otherwise the inspection is terminated and the booking wasted.
  4. Inspection (T0), then processing. TOP within 7 working days of complete documents. STD
  5. Need it faster? Express TOP: 1-working-day processing, S$500 (≤3,000 m²) / S$1,000 (>3,000 m²), subject to BCA acceptance and payment. Express Inspection: inspection within ~4 days, S$3,000 flat, subject to project size. Partial/phased TOP is routine — application area computed per phase. STD

Breaching s.12(1) or a written direction is an offence: fine up to S$50,000 and/or 12 months' imprisonment, plus up to S$2,000/day as a further offence after conviction. Practitioner-typical TOP-to-CSC duration for condos is 12–24 months TYP — the 2-year window leaves less slack than it appears once one WD cycle slips.

Part 6 — The programme & tracker

This is the working tool. Pick your project type and the programme adapts — landed drops the BCA inspection entirely (self-certification since Dec 2020) and sheds CONQUAS, lifts and B-score; industrial adds the JTC lessor layer and MOM WSH registration; data centres add the grid-connection critical path, EMA licensing, diesel storage, clean-agent CoCs and cooling-tower regimes. Anchor the programme on your target TOP inspection date — or on your target CSC date, and it derives the TOP date from an adjustable TOP→CSC allowance — and every milestone dates itself backward from completion. Every lead time is editable (days relative to T0, negative = before); section headers show how long each phase spans. Status and notes save automatically in this browser (nothing is uploaded anywhere); use Export to move a project between machines or to back it up. Print for site meetings — the dated programme prints as shown.

Offsets are calendar-day planning positions (defaults from Part 1 with their SLA/STD/TYP basis). Rows highlight when their planned date is past and the item is not Done. The T0 row is fixed at 0 — it is the anchor everything else is measured from. If you anchor on CSC, the allowance defaults to 540 days (planned TOP→CSC duration well inside the 2-year window) and stays synced with the "Target CSC application" row. The allowable-window row is computed as 2 years (730 days) after the TOP-issued row, so it moves if you adjust the TOP-issue lead — it is not directly editable.

Waivers in one paragraph

There is no waiver-at-TOP. Every track — BCA s.6A/reg 20 (building regulations, incl. accessibility, ETTV, buildability), SCDF s.59 (fire; cost alone never a ground; compensatory measures expected), CD shelter deviations, URA development-control waivers, PUB COP deviations — is designed to be exercised at or before plan approval. BCA's TOP checklist asks whether all waivers have been obtained. Anything surfacing at final inspection is rectified or regularised (record plans for immaterial deviations; amendment plans for material ones; regularisation submissions from ~S$2,500 to >S$20,000 for unauthorised works, plus URA civil penalties), never waved through.

Data centres — the heaviest overlay

Everything in the master schedule applies, plus: state capacity allocation before anything (DC-CFA award conditions: PUE ≤1.25 and Green Mark DC Platinum under CFA2; the draft Digital Infrastructure Bill of Jul 2026 will license all DCs); 66 kV+ grid connection with ~21–26 months from Consumer Connection Agreement to energisation — plan the whole programme around this, not around BCA; EMA electrical-installation licence (>45 kVA); genset licensing (island-mode <10 MW standby sets exempt; grid-synchronised schemes are not); SCDF P&FM diesel-storage licence (~19 wd incl. inspection); clean-agent suppression in data halls (Fire Code Cl. 6.5.2 relief) with the CoC chain compiled at procurement; NEA cooling-tower registration + Legionella testing regime; PUB Water Efficiency Management Plan (≥60,000 m³/yr). Delivery is shell-and-core: base-build TOP (often phased), then data-hall fit-outs as A&A cycles with their own completions. Level-5 integrated systems testing competes for the same commissioned-systems window as the statutory inspections — coordinate the calendars.
