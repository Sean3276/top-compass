The TOP critical path, assembled in one place

Every clearance below is public knowledge — scattered across the Building Control Act, seven agencies' pages and a decade of circulars. This page is the assembled dependency graph, current to the CORENET X era. For the sector with the most demanding completion profile, see the dedicated data centre section.

Critical path to TOP

The pattern: utilities gate fire, fire gates everything. Everything else runs in parallel — until it doesn't.

  1. Permanent utilities turn-on SP permanent electricity (LEW CoC + SP inspection) and PUB water turn-on (fire mains/hydrants live). Both gate testing & commissioning. For new residential developments >10 units, the SP Services (MSSL) Notice of Unit Inspection Completion is itself a TOP prerequisite.
  2. Test & commission fire systems and lifts Fire protection T&C needs live water and permanent power; lift commissioning needs permanent power. Missing Certificates of Conformity for regulated fire-safety products surface here — too late. Check them at procurement.
  3. Registered Inspector → SCDF TFP/FSC; lift PTOs Owner-appointed RI issues the Certificate of Inspection before the QP applies to SCDF (response ~3 working days). The TFP carries TOP when only very minor fire items are outstanding. Every lift/escalator needs a BCA Permit to Operate via LEAP — since 1 Oct 2025, QP certification of fixed installations is a PTO prerequisite.
  4. Parallel workstreams close PUB sanitary/drainage/water clearances · NEA and URA technical clearances · Green Mark as-built score (≥2,000 m² GFA) · as-built Buildable Design Score (≥5,000 m²; submit ~2 months ahead) · CD shelter commissioning (book ≥5 working days ahead) · CONQUAS/Quality Mark — since 1 Jun 2025 BCA will not even conduct the TOP inspection until assessments are complete and site conditions acceptable.
  5. BCA TOP inspection → TOP Key forms (Certificate of Supervision, QP declaration) lodged ≥3 days before inspection. BCA processes a complete application within 7 working days; Express TOP in 1 working day (S$500/S$1,000); Express Inspection within ~4 days at S$3,000.
  6. CSC within 2 years LTA street works, NParks greenery, the FSC proper, survey plans and residual items close out at CSC. For TOPs issued on/after 1 Jan 2023, the 2-year window is enforced through written directions attached to the TOP.
Don't book prematurely. BCA has said it twice in circulars (APPBCA-2023-03, APPBCA-2024-20): inspections on unready sites are terminated, and since 1 Jun 2025 unacceptable site conditions or incomplete CONQUAS stop the inspection from happening at all.

Published inspection service standards (BC-2025-01)

BCA circular BC-2025-01 (1 Dec 2025) formalised, for the first time, the technical agencies' service standards for TOP/CSC inspection scheduling and post-inspection response. Partially extracted figures: URA 7 working days; NParks 7 + 7; PUB 14; LTA 20 working days end-to-end. Response standards are per-agency — consult Annex A of the circular for the authoritative table before planning around any single figure.
